Answer: Muscular dystrophy (MD) is primarily caused by genetic mutations that affect the proteins responsible for healthy muscle structure and function. The altered genes disrupt the production of proteins like dystrophin, leading to progressive muscle weakness and degeneration.

Answer: No, muscular dystrophy (MD) is not contagious. It is a genetic disease caused by changes in a person's DNA, and it is not passed from person to person through contact.
